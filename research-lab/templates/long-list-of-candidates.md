# Long list of candidate paper directions (parking lot)

These are the directions we did **not** select for this 12-month round. They are kept here so they don't fall out of memory and can be picked up next quarter or by future collaborators.

Selection criteria for this round were:
1. Anchored to a written invitation from Jeff (existing paper to extend, "under development" project to build, or published framework to operationalize).
2. Spans at least one of: hardware, curriculum, HCI/AI.
3. Achievable as first author with mentor-only feedback.
4. Pairs naturally with a high-production-value YouTube companion video.

---

## C4. Image Morphs &amp; Warps as a CS1 → Linear Algebra Onramp
**Why interesting.** The Math 2BL doc lists this as a project but with no academic paper. Affine and projective transforms are the "first contact" with matrices for many CS students.
**Likely contribution.** A web-native (no install) image-warping lab + a validated affine-transform fluency rubric.
**Why deferred.** Less novel than P2/P3 — the core ideas are well-trodden in computer graphics education. Better as a side project for the YouTube channel.

## C5. Eigenfaces Lab Pipeline + an SVD Intuition Instrument
**Why interesting.** Eigenfaces is a classical SVD demo and Jeff lists it. The contribution would be a validated pre/post instrument measuring SVD intuition.
**Likely contribution.** Free Python/JS Eigenfaces lab + a 12-item validated instrument.
**Why deferred.** The instrument design is a paper in its own right and would compete for attention with P2's NNML instrument. Revisit after P2 is submitted.

## C6. P-Block 2.0 — Sub-$15 Bench Power Supply
**Why interesting.** The P-block is the binding-constraint enabler of LANA. A redesign that drops cost in half would enable adoption at high schools.
**Likely contribution.** Engineering report — schematics, BOM, safety review, classroom test data.
**Why deferred.** This is engineering, not learning sciences. Better fit for an electrical engineering co-author or for Jeff to lead with Henry as second author. Park.

## C7. Computer Graphics &amp; 3D Animations Project (also "under development")
**Why interesting.** Literally listed as "under development" in the Math 2BL doc, and naturally pairs with the Manim animation skills Henry will build for the companion videos.
**Likely contribution.** A Manim-based lab where students implement the rotation/projection/perspective pipeline themselves — turning the YouTube animation toolchain into the curriculum.
**Why deferred.** Strong second-quarter pickup. Will reuse all the Manim infrastructure built for the P1/P2 videos. Plan to start in month 6 if P1 is on track.
